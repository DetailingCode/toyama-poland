The values shown are the maximum charging voltage (bulk/absorption phase), not the battery'"'"'s operating voltage. The charger temporarily supplies a higher voltage, which — after charging finishes and a few hours of rest — settles on its own to a lower resting voltage. Gel and AGM batteries should not be discharged below 11V, as this negatively affects their lifespan.

Applications

Where carbon technology excels

Off-grid solar power

The carbon addition on the negative electrode reduces sulfation under partial-charge conditions — the typical operating state in solar installations.

Wind energy storage

Higher charge acceptance and less plate corrosion under variable, irregular energy production.

UPS backup power

Float operation with continuous trickle charging — exactly the scenario lead-carbon technology was designed for.

Frequent charge/discharge cycles

Better charge acceptance than classic lead-acid batteries under partial-cycle operation.

Electric carts and industrial vehicles

High resistance to cyclic discharge while retaining a long, engineered service life.

Boats and yachts

Maintenance-free on-board power resistant to moisture and cyclic use while cruising.

Advantages

Why this series

01

Long service life

These batteries handle cyclic operation better than traditional lead-acid batteries. Service life is engineered for around 20 years (over 2000 cycles at 80% DOD*)

02

Greater sulfation resistance

Innovative technology prevents plate sulfation, the main cause of degradation in traditional lead-acid batteries

03

Maintenance-free operation

Lead-carbon batteries are completely maintenance-free — no need to top up electrolyte or regularly check fluid levels.

04

High operating safety

Sealed construction eliminates the risk of electrolyte leaks. Minimal gas emission — ideal for alarm systems, UPS, solar installations, and electrical equipment.
